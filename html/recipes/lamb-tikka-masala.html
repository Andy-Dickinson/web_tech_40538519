Lamb Tikka Masala

  • A delicious lamb curry, perfect for home comfort (just donâ€™t think about the calories too much!).

    Best with lamb neck fillets, but works with any meat (chicken/beef/prawns) or any cut of lamb.

    Serve piping hot and pair with steamed basmati rice and a naan.

    Course: Dinner

    Prep time: 30

    Cook time: 105

    Total time: 135

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Curry Base Sauce - see link
  • 1 Tbsp Ghee
  • 2 Ibs Lamb - Cubed
  • 2 Tbsp Ground Turmeric
  • 2 Tbsp Vegetable Oil
  • 3 cloves Garlic - Peeled & Crushed
  • 1 inch Ginger - Peeled & diced
  • 1 tsp Dried Onion Powder
  • 1 tsp Ground Cumin
  • 1 tsp Ground Coriander
  • 1 tsp Paprika
  • 2 tsp Curry Powder
  • 1 tsp Dried Fenugreek Leaves
  • 6 Tbsp Tomato Paste
  • 4 Tbsp Ground Almonds
  • 1 Tbsp Brown Sugar
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 cup Single Cream
  • 6 Tbsp Corriander - Finely chopped

Method

  1. Heat the ghee in a large pan.
  2. Cover the lamb with the turmeric and brown on all sides.
  3. Pour in the curry base sauce, stir and cover with a lid.
  4. Simmer on a low heat until the meat is cooked and tender (approx. 90 minutes), stiring every 15 minutes. Then set aside.
  5. Heat the oil in a large frying pan.
  6. Fry the garlic and ginger on a low heat for 1 minute - watch not to burn.
  7. Pour the cooked lamb and its sauce into the pan and stir.
  8. Add the onion powder, cumin, coriander, paprika, curry powder, fenugreek, tomato paste, ground almonds and brown sugar to the lamb. Stir and let the curry simmer slowly for 10 minutes.
  9. Pour in the cream and garam masala. Stir well and let it simmer for another 5 minutes.
  10. Garnish with the corriander and serve with rice and naan. Enjoy!
